Abstract


The Laplace-Beltrami operator has established itself in the field of non-rigid shape retrieval due to its many useful properties such as being invariant under isometric transformation, having a countable eigendecomposition forming an orthornormal basis, and fully characterizing geodesic distances of the manifold. However, this invariancy only applies under isometric deformations, which leads to a performance breakdown in many real-world applications. In recent years emphasis has been placed upon learning optimal descriptors using deep learning methods, which take noise and non-rigid deformations into account. In this paper we take a step back, revisiting the LBO and proposing a supervised way to learn a Riemannian metric weighting layer from metrics derived from the second fundamental form of a manifold. By doing this, we can drastically reduce the spectral distance of a manifold and its deformations, resulting in an operator which is more robust and invariant to non-rigid deformations and noise. The optimization of the LBO leads to enormous improvements to established descriptors such as the heat or wave kernel signature. As many non-rigid shape retrieval algorithms rely on spectral descriptors, we outperform on established benchmarks by using our proposed optimized LBO.
